Information and Analysis

The visualisations in this project are attempting to visualise the relationship between the amount of air pollution (2.) produced and amount of cars per 1000 people in the country (1.) via the formula below .



Thousands of Tonnes of air pollution / Number of cars per 1000 people (Motorisation rate)


While the general fall in air pollution is transfered to the graph it does show that in some country's traffic does provide a greater proportion of total emissions than others.


Air pollution measurements

Particulate matter(PM 10 & PM 2.5)

Sum of all solid and liquid particles suspended in air many of which are hazardous, caused by the combustion of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and petrol. (3.)

Relationship : high

Ammonia (NH3)

NH3 contributes to acid deposition and eutrophication, which in turn, can lead to potential changes occurring in soil and water quality. 90% of emissions are the result of agriculture. (5.)

Relationship : low

Non-methane volatile organic compound (NMVOC)

Non-methane volatile organic compounds (NMVOCs) are a collection of organic compounds that differ widely in their chemical composition but display similar behaviour in the atmosphere. NMVOCs contribute to the formation of ground level (tropospheric) ozone and some are directly hazardous to human health. (4.)

Relationship : medium

Nitrogen oxides (NOX)

Result of endothermic reaction between Nitrogen and Oxygen during combustion processes, at high temperatures. (3.)

Relationship : high

Sulphur Oxides (SOX)

Emitted when fuels containing sulphur are consumed. (3.)

Relationship : high
